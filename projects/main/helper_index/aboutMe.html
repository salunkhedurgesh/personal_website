I am a postdoctoral researcher at LASA lab, EPFL, working with Prof. Aude Billard. My research spans kinematic intelligence for safe and explainable robot learning, transfer learning with sub-domain guarantees, Conformal Geometric Algebra for kinematic analysis, and the design of mechanically intelligent robots, including tensegrity-inspired joints and rolling-kinematics platforms.

I completed my PhD at LS2N, Nantes, on “Cuspidal Robots: theoretical study, classification and applications to commercial robots” under Prof. Philippe Wenger and Prof. Damien Chablat. My doctoral work established systematic frameworks for classifying cuspidal robots and their implications for motion planning in commercial cobots.

I hold a double Master’s degree from the Erasmus Mundus EMARO+ program, with a thesis on the optimal kinematic design of a parallel mechanism for otological surgery, conducted with CHU Nantes, DFKI Germany, and UNIGE Genoa.

I actively contribute to the kinematics research community, currently preparing course material for IEEE RAS University and a kinematics-grounded robot motion planning course at EPFL.

I strongly believe in,
“Progress in robotics will come from a principled fusion of learning methods with analytical methods. The future belongs to co-designed robots that are mechanically intelligent by design and autonomously adaptive by learning.”
