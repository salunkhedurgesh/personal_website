Chapter 8 - Endoscopic robotic system

Authors: Damien Chablat, Guillaume Michel, Philippe Bordure and Durgesh Haribhau Salunkhe,
In: Robot Design, 2026, doi: 10.1016/B978-0-443-24778-1.00007-2

Endoscopic surgery of the ear and sinuses promotes minimally invasive approaches and allows visualization of hard-to-reach areas. However, these procedures can be challenging because they require the surgeon to hold the endoscope, thereby immobilizing one hand. Therefore, designing an assistive robot for this type of endoscopic surgery should improve ergonomics for the surgeon and enhance patient outcomes. To precisely define the specifications of such a robot, it is essential to study the anatomical regions of the ear and sinuses. This involves creating a geometric atlas based on CT scans to determine the dimensions and variations of these robotic workspaces. Additionally, considering the needs of surgeons is a critical part of this study. The design phase of the robot begins with an analysis of various architectural choices, focusing on variations of a mechanism with an offset center of rotation. This mechanism combines a 2-DOF spherical mechanism with a double parallelogram. Finally, the modalities for controlling this robot via image tracking are defined using an algorithm that employs CamShift.
Keywords: Endoscope holder, ENT surgery, Radiology, Robot design, Robotics

Optimisation of parallel mechanisms with joint limits and collision constraints

Authors: Durgesh Haribhau Salunkhe, Guillaume Michel, Shivesh Kumar, Marcello Sanguineti and Damien Chablat,
In: Biologically Inspired Series-Parallel Hybrid Robots, 2024, doi: 10.1016/B978-0-32-388482-2.00031-7

In this chapter, a new optimization methodology for parallel kinematic manipulator (PKM) is proposed, addressing constraints related to singularities, passive joint limits, and self-collisions. The proposed optimization approach combines a local search method (Nelder-Mead algorithm) with global search methodologies such as low discrepancy distribution. This combination allows for faster and more efficient exploration of the optimization space. The algorithm optimizes a global kinematic quality along with the length of the prismatic actuators. The design constraints can be introduced modularly. This allows for a better understanding of the impact of specific criteria on the final result. The chapter also discusses the effect of the dimension of the search space. It is shown that initial knowledge on PKM can help reduce the dimension of the search space and result in more intuitive results. The presented approach is applied to optimize a PKM with a motion constraint generator of 2 degrees of freedom. This case study demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methodology in addressing the challenges associated with the optimization of PKMs.
Keywords: parallel mechanisms, optimization, Nelder-Mead
